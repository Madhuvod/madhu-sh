A quiet, devastating novel about a man who lets life happen to him. William Stoner is an English professor who loves his work, endures a hostile marriage, and never quite fights for the things that matter most. It's one of those books that sits with you long after you finish it.

## What I took away

- **Don't be passive when something truly matters.** Speak up, confront problems early, and fight for important relationships or opportunities instead of quietly accepting situations that harm your life.

- **Protect the rare things that give life meaning.** Deep relationships, intellectual passion, and work you genuinely care about are uncommon. Don't trade them away too easily for comfort, reputation, or social pressure.

- **Design your life intentionally instead of drifting.** Make conscious choices about career, relationships, and values so circumstances don't slowly decide your life for you.
